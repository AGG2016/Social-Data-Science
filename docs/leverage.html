Outliers: Assessment of Influence

Leverage

\[h_i=\frac{1}{n}+\frac{(x_i-\bar{x})^2}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i-\bar{x})^2}\]

Interpretation

  • Points close to the horizontal center of the scatterplot have low leverage, while points far from the horizontal center of the plot have high leverage.
  • Depends solely on the explanatory variable.
  • Observations with a high leverage -extreme observations of the explanatory variable-, may or may not have a considerable influence on the slope: that depends on their value in the response variable.

  geom_smooth(method = "lm") + 
  geom_point(x=88900.000,y=1578320000.0,col="blue") +
  geom_text(aes(label=ifelse(milex>85000,
  as.character(stateabb),"")),vjust=-0.5,size=2)

In the case above, the observation with the highest leverage, corresponding to Russia, stands away from the regression line and therefore is called an influencial.

Strictly speaking, the combination of high leverage and high residual -long distance from the regression line-, makes an influential.

Thus, influencials depends on the explanatory variable but also on the residual, that is, in the real and fitted values for the response variable.
